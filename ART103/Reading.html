Reading #1 Part 1: Questions And Answers

Q1. -- What is a good working definition of what a System is and perhaps what it is not?

A regularly interacting or interdependent group of items forming a unified whole,
such as an assemblage of substances that is in or tends to equilibrium. a group of
devices or artificial objects or an organization forming a network especially for
distributing something or serving a common purpose.

Q3 -- Give us your defination of what Tesslation means and give an example you
encountered, not mentioned in the reading.

A tessellation is a pattern of shapes that fit together through a system of geometric patterns.
They can be used in many architectures and geometries.

Q4 -- What is the difference between ideas of Modularity and Tesselation? What are
the properties that are in oppostion with each other?

Modularity componants of a system that can be arranged in a variety of configuations
or roles (very interchangeable)
Tessellation: When a geometric shape is repeated over and over again, covering a
plane of tiles without any gaps or overlaps, it results in a tessellation - a mosaic
pattern of a mesmerizing visual effect.

Q5 -- What is the difference between designing something that has Complexity (aperiodic)
vs being Uniform (periodic)

A signal that repeats its pattern over a period is called periodic signal. A signal that
does not repeats its pattern over a period is called aperiodic signal or nonperiodic.
