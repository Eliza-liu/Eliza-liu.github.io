Childhood Memory: Temari Ball


When I was very young, my grandmother always gave me a Temari ball that she made for my birthday.
This kind of ball represents the moral of good happiness. I like it very much. It meant a lot to
me as a child. But when I grew up, my grandma could not spend such a long time making a bow-ball.
So I learned from the Internet and did it. I hope I can give it to my grandma when I return home.

I wound the cotton with a white thread to form a ball, then measured the circumference, calculated
the position of each petal, and began to embroider the innermost petal with a needle. The process of
sleeve petal is from inside to outside. After embroidering both sides twine the line of cyan in the
middle as adornment.
